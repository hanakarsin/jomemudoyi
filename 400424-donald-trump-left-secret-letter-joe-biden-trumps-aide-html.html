    Donald Trump left secret letter to Joe Biden, says Trump's aide

    It has been confirmed that the immediate former US President Donald Trump left a secret letter for his successor, Joe Biden before he left the White House for Maryland and later Florida.

    The disclosure was made on Wednesday, January 20, by one of Trump's aides, Judd Deere, who worked with him in the White House, Yahoo.com reported.

    Speaking on the write-up by his former principal, Deere said: "It’s a letter between 45 and 46." By this, he meant it was a secret letter from the 45th to the 46th president of the US.

    As the US geared to the inauguration Wednesday, speculations were rife that angry Trump had left the White House without leaving a note for his successor, which is against the tradition whenever there is a transition.

    Trump became the first exiting US of Head of State to snub inauguration of his successor since 1869.

    He jetted out of the White House at around 4.15pm (EAT) alongside first lady Melania Trump and landed at Joint Base Andrews, Maryland at around 4.30pm.

    Trump addressed his supporters at Joint Base Andrews for the last time as president where he said: "he will be back in some form".

    "What we have done has been amazing by any standard. We have built the military, we created a new force, we were not a regular administration. We have been given very high approval rates than never before. The stock market is even at a higher point than it was pre-COVID-19. We have built it twice.

    As Trump flew to Florida aboard Air Force One, Biden and Kamala Harris were sworn in as new holders of the president and vice president offices respectively.

    In his first speech as US president, Biden called for peace, end of racism and concerted efforts to fight the COVID-19 pandemic which has in then one year, claimed lives of over 400,000 Americas.

    "This is not just a celebration of an individual but of a course. I ask every American to join me in this course...fighting anger, resentment, joblessness and diseases. We can deliver justice once again if we work together. Racism has for long torn us apart. We can see each other not as adversaries but as neighbours.
