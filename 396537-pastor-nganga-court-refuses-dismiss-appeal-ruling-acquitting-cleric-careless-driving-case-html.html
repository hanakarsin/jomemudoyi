    Pastor Ng'ang'a: Court refuses to dismiss appeal against ruling acquitting cleric from careless driv

    - Pastor Ng'ang'a alongside his co-accused had made a request to the court seeking to stop an appeal that challenged their acquittal in 2018 - The said the appeal was incompetent and was filed 14 days after the ruling that freed them

    28 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - Pastor Ng'ang'a alongside his co-accused had made a request to the court seeking to stop an appeal that challenged their acquittal in 2018

    - The said the appeal was incompetent and was filed 14 days after the ruling that freed them

    - However, the court declined their prayer and instead urged the DPP to proceed and make an application

    Controversial city pastor James Ng'ang'a has suffered a blow after the High Court refused to dismiss an appeal by the state which challenged his acquittal from his careless driving case.

    Ng'ang'a alongside three others had made a request seeking for the dismissal of the appeal on the ground of incompetence adding it was filed 14 days after his acquittal.

    However, according to a report by Daily Nation, Justice Mary Kasango said the cleric's concerns about the appeal should be raised in its final hearing.

    The judge also allowed the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) to proceed with the appeal though it had not yet been admitted by the court as it was filed later that 14-day period of the ruling.

    She said the court could still consider the appeal if the DPP makes an application to the court.

    "The court is empowered by Section 349 Criminal Procedure Code to admit an appeal filed out of time, that is an appeal already filed and in existence but to do so the court has to be moved by an application," the judge said.

    Ng'ang'a's team told the court the appeal was lodged on May 22, 2018, while his ruling was made on May 2, 2018, by Limuru Chief Magistrate Godfrey Oduor.

    However, Justice Rasanga noted there was a confusion on the exact dates because though the court freed them on May 2, it still gave reasons for their acquittal on May 7, the same year.

    She thus urged the DPP to make an application for the admittance of the appeal in the court.

    The televangelist was said to have been driving his Range Rover on the wrong side of the Naivasha-Nairobi highway when the vehicle collided with another that had two passengers back in July 2015.

    A woman, Mercy Njeri, died in the incident.

    He was charged alongside his driver Simon Maina Kuria and police officers Christopher Nzilu Nzioka and Patrick Kahindi Baya (Tigoni traffic commandant) who were accused of obstructing justice.
