    Photos: Iconic Mombasa's Mamba Village statue destroyed

    - Mombasa leaders have protested the destruction of a 41-year-old crocodile statue at the entrance of Mamba Village. The statue was brought down to pave way for road construction -Nyali MP Hezron Awiti pledged to give KSh 500,000 for the reconstruction of the structure

    02 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - Mombasa leaders have protested the destruction of a 41-year-old crocodile statue at the entrance of Mamba Village. The statue was brought down to pave way for road construction

    - Nyali MP Hezron Awiti pledged to give KSh 500,000 for the reconstruction of the structure 

    Leaders in Mombasa county have condemned the destruction of one of the region's iconic landmark.

    Nyali MP Hezron Awiti blamed the county government and a road constructor for destroying the crocodile sculpture outside Mamba Village.

    READ ALSO: Joho puts aside his latest woes and unveils ambitious KSh33 billion plan

    Mombasa county executive for transport and infrastructure Tawfiq Balala said they were equally shocked by the destruction of the Mamba Village statue.

    Both Awiti and Balala told the paper that they would liaise with Mamba Village to re-erect it. Awiti pledged KSh 500,000 for the Mamba Village statue reconstruction.

    The eight-metre long crocodile sculpture is said to have been designed and built by an Israeli architect 41 years ago.
