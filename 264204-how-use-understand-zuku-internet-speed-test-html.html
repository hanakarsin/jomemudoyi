    Zuku is a well-known East African brand, established primarily to provide internet, telephone, and satellite television services at affordable prices. The brand has snowballed because of its reliable services, mainly in providing internet services to offices and homes. But have you undergone the frustration of a slow internet connection? Do you know how to carry out your Zuku internet speed test to measure the performance of your connection?

    With the Zuku internet speed test, you can see how fast the download and upload speeds of your connection are, as well as other statistical details such as ping and jitter. The speed test will enable you to diagnose the reason why your internet connection is not performing as it should be doing. You are recommended to take multiple tests before you can settle on the speed of your connection.

    How do I check my Zuku internet speed?

    Internet speeds vary at different times of the day, depending on the number of people connected to the internet and their usage. To get a reliable gauge of your speed, whether, at your business or home, it is better to do the test at different times, during, and after peak periods.

    Before you run your Zuku WiFi speed test, it is better if you check that there is no other device or person connected to the internet. Checking this will prevent other devices on the connection from affecting your results. If it is possible, you will get a much accurate result if you connect your PC directly to the router instead of measuring over WiFi as your device may also interfere with the result.

    Below are the guidelines on how to test Zuku internet speed.

  • Close all running applications and turn off all the devices that are using the internet. Testing, while so many devices are connected, could give a result lower than expected.
  • If you are using any VPN, it is better to disconnect it as it also slows the internet speed.
  • Use a PC connected to the router via Ethernet cable. Testing over WiFi tends to give inaccurate results.
  • Now open the Zuku speed test page.
  • On the drop-down menu, below the GO button, select Zuku as your HOST.
  • Press the GO button to run speed test Zuku. Repeat the process multiple times to account for any experienced fluctuations in speed.

    Understanding your Zuku internet speed test results

    Below are the terminologies used when measuring the speed of your internet connection.

    • Ping: a ping test, is also known as the latency test. It measures how long a packet of data takes to complete a round trip between two given points. Ping is measured in milliseconds (ms).
    • Jitter: a jitter test will measure the variation over time between when your computer receives a data packet from the server. High jitter values cause the delivery of voice packets to be out of order, thereby resulting in talk-over effects or echoes. Jitter is also measured in milliseconds (ms).
    • Download: it is the speed at which data is transmitted to your PC through your internet connection. The download speed is measured in megabits per second (Mbps). Download speed denotes the largest volume of data your PC can receive at a given duration of time.
    • Upload: this is the speed at which data from your PC can travel to the internet. Upload speed is the opposite of download speed and is also measured in megabits per second. Having a good upload speed is essential when sharing large file sizes.

    Why is Zuku internet not working?

    It is frustrating to use slow internet; it gets worse when your internet connection can barely function at all. If you are using extremely slow internet, you will experience websites not loading correctly, or videos taking long to buffer. Some of the factors contributing to your Zuku internet not working may include but not limited to, the following.

    • Sometimes Zuku international traffic carrier goes down, leading to an internet outage.
    • You may have an IP conflict on your computer, so ensure that you automatically set your IP to acquire IP addresses automatically.
    • If you experience weak WiFi signals, it might be due to your house construction, such as the thickness of the walls, or because of poor placement.
    • Check where the router is placed. Avoid placing it in a locked compartment, but, put it in a central and elevated place with antennae’s raised.
    • Your router’s power adapter may not be appropriately plugged to the power outlet. Plug the adapter well and switch on the power outlet.
    • If you are connected via a WiFi extender, ensure the cable connecting both ends of the router and extender are patched well. Or, try changing the Ethernet cable.

    How do I activate Zuku WiFi?

    To activate your Zuku WiFi, you will need to pay for whichever package you have subscribed to. To avoid disconnection, it is always advisable to make your payments before the expiry of your current subscription. Alternatively, if your subscription is still running, try to reboot your router or modem, and it should work.

    How do I reset Zuku after payment?

    Sometimes for whatever reason, you may fall behind in renewing your subscription. A delayed payment always results in termination of the services offered, and in this case, you will be disconnected from the internet.

    But, once you pay for the services, you will immediately get the internet back. But if it delays, you need to call the Zuku customer care desk for them to reset your account and receive the internet again.
