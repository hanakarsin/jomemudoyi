    Highest currency in Africa in 2024: Top 12 countries revealed

    A currency value is determined by the supply and demand of goods or services in a market economy. The government regulates supply and demand factors through monetary and fiscal policy. A currency's value is important because it determines a country's economic performance. Which is the highest currency in Africa in 2024?

    Even though Nigeria has the continent's largest economy and the highest nominal GDP, dozens of African currencies are more valuable than the naira. Read on to discover the top 12 most valuable currency in Africa.

    Highest currency in Africa in 2024

    The currencies of these countries will be compared to the US Dollar, the world's strongest currency, and the Nigerian Naira, the local currency. These are the exchange rate as of November 8th 2023.

    12. Egyptian Pound

    • ISO code: EGP
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 24.34 EGP

    Egypt's national currency, the Egyptian Pound, has been used for over 200 years. Egypt is a populous African country in the continent's northeast. Egypt has one of the world's oldest economies, and its financial sector is one of the largest in Africa. One Egyptian Pound trades for about 18.07 Nigerian Nairas.

    11. Lesotho Loti

    • ISO code: LSL
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 17.85 LSL

    The Loti is the national currency of Lesotho. It was first issued as a non-circulating currency in 1966. LSL coins come in denominations of 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, and 50 lisente, as well as 1, 2, and 5 Loti. One Lesotho Loti trades for about 24.64 Nigerian Nairas.

    10. Namibian Dollar

    • ISO code: NAD
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 17.85 NAD

    The Namibian Dollar has been the currency of Namibia since 1993. The dollar replaced the South African Rand, which had been the country's currency while it was under South African rule from 1920 until 1990. One Namibian Dollar trades for about 24.64 Nigerian Nairas.

    9. South African Rand

    • ISO code: ZAR
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 17.68 ZAR

    South Africa has one of Africa's most powerful economies. Mining, tourism, and financial services are all important industries in the economy. Gold has been the country's primary export for many years. The rand is one of Africa's strongest currencies thanks to recent economic progress. One South African Rand trades for about 24.88 Nigerian Nairas.

    8. Zambian Kwacha

    • ISO code: ZMW
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 16.30 ZMW

    Zambia is a country in Southern Africa that is landlocked. The Zambian Kwacha is one of the world's best-performing currencies against the US dollar. Since 1967, the kwacha has undergone several structural changes to support economic activity while maintaining public trust in the national currency. One Zambian Kwacha trades for about 26.98 Nigerian Nairas.

    7. Eritrean Nakfa

    • ISO code: ERN
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 15.00 ERN

    Eritrea's currency is known as the Eritrean Nakfa. The Nakfa is one of Africa's most valuable currencies and Eritrea's official legal tender. It has remained stable over time due to the government's preference for a fixed exchange rate over floating the currency. One Eritrean Nakfa trades for about 29.31 Nigerian Nairas.

    6. Seychellois Rupee

    • ISO code: SCR
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 14.42 SCR

    Seychelles' currency is the rupee, which is divided into 100 cents. It employs the abbreviations SR, SCR, and SRe. It was introduced in 1914. After independence in 1976, new 10, 25, 50, and 100 rupee notes were introduced. One Seychellois Rupee trades for about 30.51 Nigerian Nairas.

    5. Ghanaian Cedi

    • ISO code: GHS
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 14.23 GHS

    Ghana is a country in West Africa. The country's economy is well-managed and constantly improving. The Cedi is the most powerful currency in Sub-Saharan Africa and the official currency in Ghana and Togo's northern region. One Ghanaian Cedi trades for about 30.92 Nigerian Nairas.

    4. Botswana Pula

    • ISO code: BWP
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 13.24 BWP

    Botswana's currency is the Pula which means "rain" or "blessing" because rain is scarce and valuable in the country. The Pula is constructed from 100 thebe, which means "shield." One Botswana Pula trades for about 33.22 Nigerian Nairas.

    3. Moroccan Dirham

    • ISO code: MAD
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 10.91 MAD

    The Moroccan Dirham is another strong African currency and Morocco's official monetary currency. How many units is the dirham divided into? The Dirham is divided into 100 units, known as santims. They are available in nine denominations of coins and four denominations of banknotes. One Moroccan Dirham trades for about 40.35 Nigerian Nairas.

    2. Libyan Dinar

    • ISO code: LYD
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 5.00 LYD

    The Libyan Dinar is one of Africa's most valuable currencies. Libya has abundant natural resources, including oil, gold, and silver. One Libyan Dinar trades for about 88.00 Nigerian Nairas.

    1. Tunisia Dinar

    • ISO code: TND
    • Dollar exchange rate: 1 USD = 3.22 TND

    The Tunisian Dinar is the strongest African currency. It was introduced in Tunisia in 1960, replacing the Franc as the country's primary currency. Tunisia has a diverse economy that includes industry, agriculture, mining, petroleum products, and tourism, and it is one of Africa's most competitive economies. One Tunisia Dinar trades for about 136.70 Nigerian Nairas.

    Which currency is lower than the Naira?

    The naira is the currency of Nigeria. It was introduced on 1 January 1973, replacing the Nigerian pound. Currencies lower than the naira include the Sudanese pound, the Comorian franc and the Somali shilling.

    What is the weakest currency in Africa? Sierra Leone has one of the lowest and weakest currencies in the world. One USD trades for about 17,655.00 Sierra Leonean Leone.
