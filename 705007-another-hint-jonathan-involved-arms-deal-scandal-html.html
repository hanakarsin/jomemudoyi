    Jonathan Linked To Arms Deal Scandal

    One of the lawyers defending the former national security adviser Sambo Dasuki has linked ex-president Goodluck Jonathan to the current arms deal investigation.     23 Mar 2024, 00:00

    One of the lawyers defending the former national security adviser Sambo Dasuki has linked ex-president Goodluck Jonathan to the current arms deal investigation.

    [article_adwert]

    Ahmed Raji, Dasuki’s lawyer, noted that the arms deal investigation cannot be discussed without reference to the former president.

    “The national security adviser will normally carry out the instructions of his president,” he told AFP.

    READ ALSO: Presidency Vows Jonathan Will Be Prosecuted If Corrupt

    “You cannot talk about this matter without talking about President Jonathan,” Raji added.

    It is uncertain if President Buhari will prosecute his predecessor while many political experts say that Buhari will most likely not do so.

    When Buhari was elected president in 2015, he said in his acceptance speech: “President Jonathan has nothing to fear from me.”

    READ ALSO: Dasuki Releases Images Of Weapons Bought By GEJ

    But with the arraignment of a powerful member of Jonathan’s government, the ex-president could yet be dragged into the case.

    However, the presidency announced that Jonathan will be forced to face justice if he is found guilty of any corruption charges. 

    Dasuki is standing trial in different courts to answer charges bordering on the supposed diversion of $2.1 billion meant for the purchase of arms for the fight against the deadly Boko Haram sect.
