    Wanovakali are indeed talented. The name loosely translates to “those with vocals" in Swahili dialect. The group won the coveted title of Old Mutual’s Amazing Voices with a cash prize of Ksh.10 million. They were against other similarly talented singing groups from Ghana, South Africa, Zimbabwe, and Kenya. And the group, which consists of six incredibly talented vocalists, took to social media to thank their fans across the globe for their immense support. So, what is the group's story?

    Wanavokali background and rise to fame

    Wanavokali started their musical journey as background vocalists for different artists in Kenya. They officially came together during the 2018 Safaricom Twaweza Live. The group decided to share their passion for expressing art and stories through singing by forming the Acapella group that sings amazingly well.

    Their debut in the music industry was through Nyashinski’s Malaika cover, which was well-received by fans. Today, they have graduated from background singers to one of Kenya's most celebrated and famous musicians.

    Although they met three years ago, the team’s remarkable chemistry shows that they perfectly synchronize. However, it wasn't until they worked on various small projects after the Safaricom tour that they realized they have a fantastic connection.

    Wanavokali started producing covers for artists they had worked with during the Safaricom Twaweza Live Tour. This exposure allowed the Acapella group to get an impressive social media following. And considering their unique voices, they won the unequivocal support of Kenyans.

    Wanavokali songs

    After gaining incredible experience during the Safaricom Twaweza Live Tour and their careers, the group decided to create their first work after coming together. First, they participated in the Old Mutual Amazing Voices competition and won. The victory gave the Acapella group an excellent reputation in the music industry.

    They attracted elevated attention after doing a unique version of Nameless' song Salary. The cover song was shared by other famous personalities on their social media platforms, like comedian Njugush. This phenomenon gave the group a lot of traction.

    Ethic: members, rise to fame, latest songs

    Wanavokali are aware of the industry evolution, and the decision to go the Acapella route was a strategic one. They wanted to create a niche for themselves, which would stand out, and it worked pretty well.

    The members realized that the niche is untapped in Kenya, and their songs would create immense value. So they worked with gospel artist Collo G and released their first single, Unioshe. The song was well-received by the audience and even topped local charts.

    Unioshe was so great that it afforded the group a Café Ngoma nomination for Best Cross Over Act (2019-2020). In addition, in 2020, Wanavokali won The Verse 5 Awards for Collaboration of the year.

    Wanavokali has worked on various music projects during the lockdown period and collaborated with different acclaimed songwriters and producers. On top of this, they have a soon-to-be-released self-titled album that fans are eagerly awaiting.

    Ricky from Wanavokali arrest

    Ricky, one of the group's talented members, was arrested by police in plain clothes at the Globe cinema roundabout. His arrest happened when the country has a great public debate on police profiling, especially for dreadlocked individuals.

    According to fellow band members, they were crossing the road, and one of the officers allegedly grabbed the musician and demanded to frisk him. However, the police officer couldn't provide a valid reason for the random search. And this made the individuals around him believe that he was profiled because of his dreadlocks.

    Even though they protested the arrest, Ricky was still arrested and booked at the Central Police Station. The incident brought much debate online as the band members took to social media to protest the arrest.

    Fortunately, the hoard of support turned up the heat leading to the release of the musician without any charge. It is also believed that the police issued a public apology through the police station's OCS, although the statement is not public.
