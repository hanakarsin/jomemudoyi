    Uhuru Lays Foundation of MV Uhuru 2, First Ship to be Built in Kisumu after 70 Years

    - The president opened the Kenya Shipyard Limited (KSL) in Kisumu - KSL is a new Government shipbuilding and repair facility with yards in Mombasa and Kisumu - The facility will oversee the building of MV Uhuru 2 and the revamping of the Kisumu shipyard

    06 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - The president opened the Kenya Shipyard Limited (KSL) in Kisumu

    - KSL is a new Government shipbuilding and repair facility with yards in Mombasa and Kisumu

    - The facility will oversee the building of MV Uhuru 2 and the revamping of the Kisumu shipyard

    On Monday, May 31, President Uhuru Kenyatta opened the Kenya Shipyard Limited(KSL) in Kisumu.

    KSL will be responsible for shipbuilding and repair services at the Kisumu port.

    One of the projects the company will be undertaking is the construction of MV Uhuru 2 whose foundation was laid by the president accompanied by his Burundi counterpart Evariste Ndayishimiye.

    MV Uhuru 2 will be the first vessel to be built in Kisumu after 70 years.

    The Kisumu yard had been handed to the Navy department in the Kenya Defence Forces who have been rehabilitating it for the past two years.

    KSL will now take over to manage the yard.

    Other than Kisumu, KSL oversees a shipyard in Mombasa where it has been charged to refit the KNS Shupavu at the Coast.

    Refurbished MV Uhuru Cargo Ferry

    During the foundation laying exercise of the ship (MV Uhuru 2), the president also officiated the flagging off the refurbished MV Uhuru ferry that had stalled at the Kisumu port for more than a decade.

    Since February this year, MV Uhuru has been ferrying petroleum commodities to Uganda when its renovation was complete.

    Since its revival, the cargo ferry has transported more than 50 million litres of petroleum products to Jinja in Uganda, from where other regions in the great lakes including DRC and Burundi and Rwanda, have been sourcing their fuel.

    The president has been in the Nyanza region since Sunday, May 30, when he landed in Kisumu.

    Today being the second day of his three-day visit to the region, Uhuru has already commissioned a number of multi-billion projects funded by the national government in partnership with other organisations like the African Development Bank.

    The visit will reach its climax on Tuesday, June 1 when the president will lead the nation in marking the 58th Madarak Day held at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, Kisumu.
