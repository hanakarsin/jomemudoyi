    Governor Sonko allows reconstruction of lavish CBD car wash 2 months after demolition

    The controversial Pimp My Ride car wash located along Kenyatta Avenue in the Nairobi CBD is under construction again, TUKO.co.ke has learnt. Construction for the luxury business premises is underway barely two months after its demolition which was justified by the Nairobi county government.

    07 Mar 2024, 00:00

    The controversial Pimp My Ride car wash located along Kenyatta Avenue in the Nairobi CBD is under construction again, TUKO.co.ke has learnt.

    Construction for the luxury business premises is underway barely two months after its demolition which was justified by the Nairobi county government.

    Nairobi county officials had in May 27 issued a statement saying the structures were illegal and had to be brought down.

    The developer was accused of failing to follow the correct avenues when establishing the car wash and other businesses which lay on the land previously occupied by the famous Simmers Restaurant.

    Barely two months later, however, and it looks like the Nairobi county government has made a U-turn on the decision and the car wash is under construction once again.

    Site manager James Saitoti who spoke on TUKO.co.ke on Wednesday, July 10, said that a demolition was retaliatory move by governor and that the owner was not issued with any notice to the effect.

    READ ALSO: Willy Paul claims singer Alaine ditched him, begs for forgiveness

    ”That was pure malice and we cannot suffer because our boss has a problem with the governor, No notice was issued, we lost everything. Some vehicles belonging to our customers were also destroyed, we can’t allow this to continue,” he said.

    Incidentally, the initial demolition of the business complex came at the back of Sonko’s tussle with business tycoon Patrick Nderitu who owns Pimp My Ride who the governor accused of attempting to grab a piece of land in Buruburu, Nairobi owned by a widow.

    Sonko fought some goons at Buruburu church and school where he vowed to teach Nderitu a lesson by demolishing his business at the Nairobi Central Business District.

    He went ahead and destroyed the premises and accused the businessman of illegally constructing structures without required licences from the Nairobi county government.

    Later, a recorded phone call between Sonko and Nderitu was leaked where the businessman made peace with the governor with a w warning that he should keep off the Buruburu land.

    The land where the car wash is set to re-established has always had tussles as in March 2018, the famous club Simmers was also brought down over a disagreement.
