    My Friends and Family Shunned Me Because I Married a Blind Woman, Kenyan Man Says

    They say love is blind, and perhaps Lawi Kiogora's story is the best practical example of this popular saying. Even after rejection from family and friends, Kiogora stood by his decision to marry Irene Kangai, a visually impaired woman. It was because of love, he said.

    31 Mar 2024, 00:00
    • Lawi Kiogora has opened up about his marriage into a family of visually impaired members
    • Kiogora says he and his wife Irene Kangai, who is totally blind, have two sons, and they are happy
    • The father of two recalled falling deeply in love with Kangai when he met her in 2009

    They say love is blind, and perhaps Lawi Kiogora's story is the best practical example of this popular saying.

    Even after rejection from family and friends, Kiogora stood by his decision to marry Irene Kangai, a visually impaired woman. It was because of love, he said.

    Kiogora has never forgotten the day he met his wife Irene in 2009. He fell deeply in love with her and pursued her relentlessly and was glad when she accepted him.

    He is glad he married the love of his life despite the rejection from family and friends.

    I just see her and love her

    Speaking to TUKO.co.ke's Kevin Philips Momanyi about marrying into a family where all members are visually impaired, Kiogora had only nice things to say about his wife and her family.

    "I love my wife so much. This family is blessed and will go far. My Irene and I have two sons. People at home were hesitant about our relationship but I stood my ground. My father especially was very reluctant to allow this relationship but I told him that I will not back out. Even my friends shunned me for my decision to marry a blind woman. They called me mad," he narrated, adding:"I just see her and love her."

    On her part, Irene narrated she was born with good vision, but as soon as she started school, she noticed that seeing the blackboard was a challenge. Her parents then took her to a special school, where she continued with her education.

    She recalled on the day she met her husband, she was not looking for love but help to move around.

    But when her husband Kiogora became interested in her, she noted that it was difficult to explain to him that her entire family was visually impaired. However, when she did, he accepted them just the way they were, and she wouldn't have it any other way.

    10 family members are blind

    Kangai's mom is also blind, and so is her grandfather, the father to her mom. She has two sons with Kiogora, who also have low vision children and the family lives together in Gatimbi, Meru county.

    "Basically, 10 of my family members are blind. People say we are cursed. I don't remember how my mom looks. I wish I could see my husband and kids. I love my husband so much. He is a good man and helps us a lot," she told TUKO.co.ke.

    Kangai has undergone 12 surgeries to try and help her see again, but they have all failed. Her mom, Lucy Muingwa, who is 62 years became blind in 1975 when she was 20 years old.

    Muingwa recounted how people ask her for money when they are called upon to help the family move around or do small tasks.

    I wish I could see my grandkids

    "I wish I could see my grandkid's and get to know how they look. It breaks my heart that they are also partially blind. I urge those people who can see to please use their sight for a good cause," Muingwa said.

    I know my children's voices

    Kangai said it has been difficult living with this condition, mainly because people keep saying that her family is cursed.

    Her only wish is if she could be gifted with chicken to sell eggs and eke a living from this venture.

    Kangai has never seen her kids but says she knows their voices, which are music to her ears.
