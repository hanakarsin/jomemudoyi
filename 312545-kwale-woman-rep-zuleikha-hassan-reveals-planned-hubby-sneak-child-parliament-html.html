    Kwale Woman Rep Zuleikha Hassan reveals she planned with hubby to sneak child into Parliament

    - Zuleikha said she notified her hubby about the plan upon learning their nanny had left - The husband gave her the go ahead, paving way to performance of the script at parliament - Zuleikha said she wanted to create awareness about challenges lactating mothers face at the places of work

    - Zuleikha said she notified her hubby about the plan upon learning their nanny had left

    - Zuleikha said she wanted to create awareness about challenges lactating mothers face at the places of work

    Kwale Women Representative Zuleikha Hassan has admitted she planned the baby drama that ensued in parliament on Wednesday, August 7.

    The MP who brought parliamentary activities to a standstill after walking into chambers with the infant, said she had informed her hubby about what she was going to do in the Assembly that day.

    The women rep said she arrived at the plan with her man after learning late on Tuesday, August 6, that their nanny would not be available to take care of the infant the following day, Nation reported.

    “I said this is enough. Why should I miss work just because I have a baby? I called my hubby and explained to him and he gave me the go-ahead,” said Zuleikha

    READ ALSO: Lazima Raila awe rais 2022, Babu Owino asema

    What followed was for the female politician to draw a script of how to sneak the baby into the debating chambers and drive the point home.

    Upon reaching the precincts of parliament, she properly dressed her baby in the cab she had hired and tactfully assessed for a loophole in the heavily guarded premise.

    Zuleikha said her target was to ensure she got the attention of the Speaker first, thus her entry point was to be strategic.

    She avoided the tight security at the main entrance and easily beat the 'absent-minded' orderly at the back door of the National Assembly and made it into the chambers.

    "The security team tried to stop me but it was very late. I was already in the Chamber. I went straight to the front row where I wanted the Speaker to notice me easily and started breastfeeding my little angel," she said

    To her expectations, acting Speaker Chris Omulele 'played into her trap' and ordered her out of the chambers with immediate effect.

    The incident sparked instant debate in parliament with a section of MPs coming to her defence.

    A group of women lawmakers accused the acting speaker of being insensitive to issues lactating mother face at their work place.

    READ ALSO: Vifo Tanzania: Zaidi ya 60 wahofiwa kufariki badaa ya gari la mafuta kuwaka moto Morogoro

    A section of male Mps led by Homa Bay legislator Peter Kaluma also defended Zuleikha's decision to walk with the infant into the chambers saying rights of a minor should at all times supersede any other rights.

    The stalemate lasted for close to half an hour before normal operations resumed.

    Zuleikha said that though her plan was successful, she was disappointed Omulele did not allow members to discuss the matter exhaustively as Speaker Justin Muturi would have done.

    "I believe if it were Mr Muturi, he would have allowed time for other members to have a say on the incident,” she added

    The leadership of the National Assembly was later compelled to take Zuleikha through the parliament's reserved room for lactating mothers to avoid a similar incident in the future.
