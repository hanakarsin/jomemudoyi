    The outspoken exiled lawyer Miguna Miguna has fulfilled his promise to help a bright science student who has had to do menial jobs to raise her university fees.

    The student, Esther Amimo, became a sensation in 2019 after winning an international science award but was struggling to raise her school fees for her undergraduate studies at the University of Nairobi.

    Taking to Facebook on Monday, August 23, the Canadian attorney said, through his KMM Lawyers, Amimo's tuition, rent, books, food and basic needs had been fully met.

    "Promises made, promises kept, I'm delighted to announce that the brilliant Esther Amimo Anyanzwa's University of Nairobi tuition, accommodation/rent, books, food and basic needs have been fully met by KMM Lawyers," said Miguna.

    The barrister wished her the best of luck in her studies and challenged her to get a first-class degree.

    Peter Tabichi's students

    Amimo and her counterpart Salome were globally renowned teacher Peter Tabichi's students and they each won KSh 100,000 which she said she used to enrol at UoN.

    As part of their prize for their outstanding science project, Amino and Salome were to get a scholarship overseas but that did not come to pass.

    After her plight was aired by a local publication, the vocal lawyer expressed interest in aiding the soon-to-be third-year student.

    "Please link me with this brilliant scholar, Esther Amimo and her family, so that I can help her get funding for her university education," he said.

    As reported, the Tabichi's students from Keriko Day Secondary School in Nakuru county scooped First UN Sustainable Development Goal Award for their outstanding research.

    The teacher bagged the 2019 Global Teacher Prize after beating 10,000 other colleagues from 170 countries.

    Amimo and Salome were selected among 10 finalists to represent Kenya in Phoenix, USA. The two developed a noble device for the visually impaired and the deaf learners for measuring length.
