    Concern Worldwide Kenya has been in existence for more than 50 years and has worked with the most vulnerable groups from developing countries. They have worked to reduce the suffering, hunger and poverty among the less fortunate. Concern Worldwide Kenya works with the poor directly while also using the organization’s knowledge and experience to influence key decisions on the local, national and international levels on how to reduce extreme poverty.

    Concern Worldwide Kenya traces its origin on September of 1968, when Africa Concern sent Columcille, a 600-tonne ship, to Sao Tome. The ship was full of supplies; medicine, powdered food, and batteries. The supplies were delivered the following day. That commitment became ongoing and led to the expansion of the volunteers. The Concern Africa’s operation then later materialised into one flight a day for eleven months. Over time, it was not just aid but the increased number of volunteers who have helped in a quick response with practical solutions which saw the organisation head to a new direction. In 1970 when a huge cyclone hit the eastern parts of Pakistan, which is now Bangladesh, the public was asked to respond, and they responded. It is from these small beginnings; together with visionary leaders that Concern Worldwide was born.

    Concern Worldwide Kenya has been in existence since 2002. While originally urban-based, the organization has now expanded its work in Kenya into a multi-sectoral programme that focuses on urban and rural livelihoods, water and sanitation, primary education, health and nutrition, and governance as later discussed on this article. The organisation is currently working in rural Marsabit County and slums of Nairobi. Concern Worldwide Kenya also works in collaboration with other local organisations to help in responding to various emergencies resulting from drought, floods, and violence. All its work is underpinned on the promotion of advocacy, community empowerment, and governance initiatives.

    Services offered by Concern Worldwide Kenya

    The various services offered by the organisation include;

    Emergency response

    The state of food insecurity in Marsabit is a serious one. Recently, the organisation developed a SURGE model which is working great in supporting health services and in preparation of emergencies and treatment cases of malnutrition in the region. Concern Worldwide Kenya has launched the emergency response which focuses on the provision of water and nutrition services. They are also providing Aquatabs to more than 7,500 families which make it easier for the families to purify water, hence getting enough safe water for drinking. The program will also see 1443 families receive cash payments over the next three months in Dukana ward. This money will help them buy food and medicine as well as other important things they may need. The organisation is also engaging in long term programming to enable them to build local resilience.

    Health and nutrition

    The organisation also works to improve the nutritional and health status of children. They also extend care to pregnant and lactating mothers from poor communities. The program by Concern Worldwide Kenya also offers support to health clinics in phases depending on the number of children who come for treatment. This ensures that clinics do not experience too much pressure especially when the cases are on the rise. Concern Worldwide distributed supplementary food in North Horr Sub-county, to the starving mothers. This, as a result, saw the number of cases with Severe Acute Malnutrition drop from 9.86 to 5% in 2017. Their Blanket Supplementary Feeding programme also reached 2692 pregnant and lactating women as well as 15, 084 children under the age of five.

    Education

    Concern Worldwide Kenya has an education programme aimed to increase the right to free education for children. The programme has reached over 288 schools both in Marsabit and Nairobi slums. From the report of 2017, school attendance had increased from 92.3% to 97% for boys, while that of girls increased from 92.5% to 96.8%.

    The organisation has also been involved in the publication of government approved guidelines for regulation of the informal schools which was developed in partnership with the Ministry of Education and other stakeholders. This document marked a critical step towards securing quality, free primary education for the over 100,000 less fortunate children living in the Nairobi’s informal settlement.

    Livelihood improvement

    The livelihood programme by Concern is dedicated to the extreme poor in Marsabit County as well as the Nairobi urban informal settlement. The programme has in the past developed a pro-poor cash transfer mechanism which ensured that no cost was transferred to the beneficiaries of the programme. The programme also ensured that cash transfer targeted to the women beneficiaries meant for household needs achieved the purpose and also worked great in affirming the role of women in making key family decisions.

    Concern Worldwide Kenya has also entered into an MOU (Memorandum of Understanding) with the Nairobi City Council developing an Urban Early Warning Early Action (UEWEA) response package which helped greatly in setting effective early warning and response systems.

    Governance and advocacy

    Concerns Worldwide Kenya also aims to improve current governance systems in Kenya. This is done through increased participation of Extreme Poor in Civic Life. The use of community conversations has indeed led to them being included in key decision making. For instance, Community Conversations was used to establish various Community Health Committees and Community Health Units in Marsabit. In Kisumu, a total of 125 community conversation members influenced the decision to include their community action plans on the county annual budget.
