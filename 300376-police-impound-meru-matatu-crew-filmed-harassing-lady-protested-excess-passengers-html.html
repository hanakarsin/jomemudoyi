    Police impound Meru matatu whose crew were filmed harassing lady who protested against excess passen

    - The matatu of the crew filmed harassing a passenger has been impounded - Owner of the PSV in question was also arrested, although the culprits are still at large - Sacco officials were supposed to produce the rogue crew but they reportedly went into hiding

    09 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - The matatu of the crew filmed harassing a passenger has been impounded

    - Owner of the PSV in question was also arrested, although the culprits are still at large

    - Sacco officials were supposed to produce the rogue crew but they reportedly went into hiding

    The long arm of the law has caught up with the rogue matatu involved in an outrageous incident on Sunday, March 10 where a lady was forced to cut short her journey for protesting against carrying of excess passengers.

    Reports reaching TUKO.co.ke indicate police in Meru have impounded the Matatu registered under the Meru Shuttle Limited after a video clip was widely shared on social media.

    The owner of the vehicle was also arrested.

    Soon after the video went viral on social media, the National Transport and Safety Authority issued a statement announcing they had directed the SACCO officials to present the matatu crew members to police.

    However, despite impounding the vehicle, the two - driver and his conductor - still remain at large.

    "The driver, Mr. Misheck Muriithi Gikunju and the conductor, Mr. Kevin Munene Gitau are currently at large and the Police have issued a warrant of arrest against them," a statement from the NTSA read

    The Matatu owner , identified as Phillip Kiminja, told authorities his crew went into hiding after learning authorities were after them following the mid-journey incident.

    On Monday, March 11, the lady who was victimised by the matatu crew came forward in her first imterview since the incident where she confirmed everything that was caught on video.

    According to the lady, after a brief disagreement with the conductor, the driver stopped the vehicle and ejected the lady and her son from the car for allegedly refusing to give way to excess passengers.
