    A Nigerian girl has secured 19 scholarships from different universities across in US and Canada.

    Victory Yinka-Banjo, a 17-year-old high school graduate, is elated after receiving scholarship offers to the tune of KSH 5 million.

    According to a report by CNN news, the scholarship money was for an undergraduate program.

    When Victory received the information, she struggled to internalise the news.

    To her, it was all a trial and error thing. She was not confident that she would even get a response.

    "It still feels pretty unbelievable. I applied to so many schools because I did not even think any school would accept me," Victory said.

    The teenager reiterated that she had received multiple offers from big universities and that all reassured her they were offering the best.

    The institutions that offered her scholarships in the US included; The Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Stanford University, Johns Hopkins University, and Virginia.

    The ones from Canada were the Lester B. Pearson scholarship from the University of Toronto, the Karen McKellin International Leader of Tomorrow (KMILOT) and the University of British Columbia.

    "Their admissions processes are extremely selective; they only accept the best of the best. So, you can imagine how I have to remind myself that I actually got into these schools daily," Victory added.

    The brainy student also noted that she was still doing her research on all the institutions before settling for one.

    Victory's mother, Chika Yinka-Banjo, said her daughter's story should inspire other young Nigerians who think that success can only be achieved in well-equipped and expensive schools.
