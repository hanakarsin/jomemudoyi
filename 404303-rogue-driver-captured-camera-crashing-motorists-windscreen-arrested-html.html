    A rogue matatu driver who was captured insulting a female motorist will spend the weekend at a police cell after he was arrested.

    Fredrick Otieno Owino was on Saturday, February 27, picked by a team of three police officers from Thika Police Station who pursued him to Makongeni estate in Thika.

    According to Mataara Sacco vice-chairperson Julius Muritu, Otieno went into hiding soon after the management got wind of his alleged gross misconduct, which was captured on camera.

    "We reported the matter to Kahawa West and luckily the base commander assigned three police officers to arrest him. The driver drove to Thika on, realising we were after him. The officers finally arrested him. He is in custody at the moment," he confirmed.

    In a short video, the rogue driver was seen exchanging words with an unidentified female motorist whom he dared to take a picture of him.

    Otieno has been with the Sacco for five years, then crashed her car damaging its windscreen.

    The incident happened along Kahawa West-Marurui road on Friday, February 27.

    "We want to apologise to the public because what the driver did was wrong. As a management we let everyone carry his or her own cross," the Sacco boss added.

    The officer commanding Kasarani police station Muthuri Mwongera confirmed Otieno would be arraigned and possibly charged on Monday, March 1.

    Among the charges he is likely to face will be contravening traffic laws and destruction of property.
