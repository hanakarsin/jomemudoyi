    The Council of Governors (CoG) has asked the government to quickly deploy the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) to the war-torn Laikipia county.

    In a statement issued on Thursday, September 9, CoG chair Martin Wambora said he was concerned with the sporadic incidents of insecurity in the region.

    "The council vehemently condemns the gross violation of human rights by the bandits who are causing a lot of mayhem in Laikipia county. It is equally worrying and alarming the ease with which firearms are being accessed by bandits and militants who are causing unrest to residents."

    The Embu governor warned that if the menace continues, then the country risks going back to the 1980s, where deadly attacks were the order of the day.

    The CoG boss proposed a raft of measures to end the conflicts.

    "That the national government should engage military personnel to reinforce the Kenya Police who are already on the ground. Arrest and prosecute suspects and establish a comprehensive mechanism for disarmament," Wambora said.

    Sophisticated firearms

    As the security situation in Laikipia appeared to get out of hand, it emerged that bandits were armed with sophisticated firearms.

    Rift Valley Regional Coordinator George Natembeya while leading an operation at the Laikipia Nature Conservancy on Tuesday, September 7, said the bandits own guns that cannot be matched by those used by the Kenyan police officers.

    He said that some of the weapons, like the M16 guns, are owned by foreign troops who conduct paramilitary training in the region.

    'The guns that these bandits are using are mighty. They are called M16. Most of them are only used by foreign troops who come to train in Kenya,'' Natembeya told journalists.

    He later retracted his statement and said the police had more powerful weapons.

    Gov't to help displaced residents

    Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i said government would help Laikipia families displaced by bandits.

    Matiang'i said the government was revamping security to ensure peace was restored in the region.

    At least 10 people have been killed in the clashes.
