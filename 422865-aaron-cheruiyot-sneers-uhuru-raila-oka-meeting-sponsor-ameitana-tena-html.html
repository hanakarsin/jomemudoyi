    Aaron Cheruiyot Sneers at Uhuru, Raila and OKA Leaders' Meeting: &quot;Sponsor Ameitana Tena&quot;

    Kericho senator Aaron Cheruiyot has taken a jibe at a planned meeting to be hosted by President Uhuru Kenyatta at the Mombasa State Lodge. According to reports, the president is set to host the One Kenya Alliance (OKA) principals alongside the ODM party leader Raila Odinga.

    22 Feb 2024, 00:00
    • According to reports, President Uhuru Kenyatta will on Wednesday preside over a meeting bringing together ODM leader Raila Odinga and his counterparts from the One Kenya Alliance (OKA)
    • The meeting succeeds another one held by the parties last week
    • Their opponents claim the meeting will be a strategic one as they prepare for the 2022 General Election

    Kericho senator Aaron Cheruiyot has taken a jibe at a planned meeting to be hosted by President Uhuru Kenyatta at the Mombasa State Lodge.

    According to reports, the president is set to host the One Kenya Alliance (OKA) principals alongside the ODM party leader Raila Odinga.

    The supposed meeting has aroused curiosity in the political quarters, those against Uhuru speculating that the agenda therein could be related to the 2022 campaigns.

    "Sponsor ameitana tena..Kwani Mlikula fare?...(Their sponsor has summoned them again, have they 'eaten fair'?" said Cheruiyot in a tweet.

    Reading from the tweet sent by Cheruiyot, the said meeting could be a strategic one as the parties involved continue to prepare for the vote in 2022.

    According to the interpretation of the tweet by netizens, the Mombasa development was political as expected, given the same had happened when the president was on good terms with his deputy, William Ruto.

    OKA's resolution, Raila's roadmap

    At the start of the week, the OKA principals met in Naivasha, Nakuru county, to deliberate on their preferred presidential candidate in the next year's polls.

    The team vowed to field their own candidate despite calls for them to unite with Raila.

    OKA brings together senators Gideon Moi and Moses Wetangula of KANU and Ford Kenya respectively, Wiper's Kalonzo Musyoka and Musalia Mudavadi of ANC.

    On his part, Raila was in the same county from where he launched his roadmap to state house come next year.

    The former premier received a kingly welcome with the leaders in his company endorsing him for the country's highest office.

    After their political escapades in Nakuru, the luminaries, who were formerly in NASA, will on Wednesday meet in Mombasa under the stewardship of Uhuru, who seems to be playing a kingmaker role as he prepares to leave office next year.

    The meeting comes less than a week after another one was held by the same parties, at the same venue.

    Since the 2018 handshake between Uhuru and Raila, the former has had a soft spot for the opposition team, which he has lately preferred to succeed him.
